Pancakes

6 servings

Ingredients:

  • Flour [2 cups]
  • Sugar [3 heaping tbp]
  • Baking Powder [2 tbp]
  • Salt [3 shakes]
  • Milk [1 1/2 cups]
  • Eggs [3]
  • Vanilla Extract [1 tsp]
  • Butter [5 tbp]

Instructions:

  1. Mix the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt together in a mixing bowl.
  2. Start melting 2 tbp of butter in a pan.
  3. Add the eggs and milk to the mixing bowl and mix until smooth.
  4. Add vanilla extract and mix until fully integrated.
  5. Add melted butter from the pan.
  6. Pour pancake mix into pan until it reaches the desired size.
  7. Flip when there are more than three large bubbles in the middle of the pancake.
  8. Enjoy!

Ham and Roast Beef Sandwich

1 serving

Ingredients:

  • Bread [2 slices]
  • Hummus [1 container]
  • Persian Cucumber [1]
  • Tomatoes [1-2]
  • Ham [3-4 slices]
  • Roast Beef [3-4 slices]

Instructions:

Note: Any type of meat will do for this sandwich.

  1. If desired, toast the bread.
  2. Smear hummus on one or both pieces of bread
  3. Slice the cucumber and tomato(es) evenly.
  4. Place down the cucumbers, ham, roast beef, and tomatoes on top of the hummus before closing the sandwich with the other piece of bread.
  5. Enjoy!

Chocolate Ice Cream

# servings currently unknown

Note: This recipe was taken fromdavidlebovitz.comand tweaked due to the supplies on hand.

Ingredients:

  • Whole Milk [1 cup]
  • Corn Starch [4 tsp]
  • Heavy Cream [1 cup]
  • Evaporated Milk [1 cup]
  • Sugar [2/3 cup]
  • Light Corn Syrup [2 tbp]
  • Cocoa Powder (Unsweetened) [1/3 cup]
  • Bittersweet or Semisweet Chocolate (Chopped) [3 oz]
  • Sea Salt [1/4 tsp]
  • Vanilla or Chocolate Extract [1/2 tsp or 1 tsp]

Instructions:

Note: You will need a small bowl, a saucepan, a larger bowl, and a good amount of ice for this recipe.

  1. In a small bowl, make a slurry of a few tablespoons of milk and the cornstarch (a slurry is a mixture of a fine powder and a liquid).
  2. In the saucepan, heat the remaining milk, heavy cream, evaporated milk, sugar, and corn syrup until it is gently boiling.
  3. Whisk in the cocoa powder and allow it to boil for 4 minutes
  4. Whisk in the slurry from earlier and stir constantly for one additional minute until the mixture is slightly thickened (Note: Do not scrape at the bottom of the pan. You will get undesireable lumps in your ice cream.)
  5. As the saucepan is removed from heat, stir in the chocolate and sea salt until the mixture is completely smooth. Now add in the vanilla or chocolate extract.
  6. Immediately transfer the mixture into a Ziploc or other resealable bag and submerge in a large bowl filled with ice. Let sit for about 30 minutes (Note: I had less ice so I put it in the freezer and let it sit for 45 minutes rather than 30 minutes).
  7. Finally, remove the bag from the bowl/freezer and scoop contents into the ice cream maker, following the manufacturer's directions.
  8. Enjoy!
